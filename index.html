Our Guiding Light

The Strong Humans Foundation is a dedicated non-profit organization committed to empowering veterans, first responders, and their family and friends. Our goal is to provide opportunities for veterans and first responders to strengthen relationships with whoever they choose - whether family, friends, or loved ones. This inclusive approach sets us apart, as we believe healing happens best when shared with those who matter most. We foster healthy living and strong relationships in the aftermath of traumatic brain injuries (TBI) and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) through natural and holistic treatment methods and activities.

Our Mission

We synchronize and enhance already established 501(c)(3) organizations through retreats utilizing different modalities of treatment through holistic and natural means. Our approach combines therapeutic activities - from equine therapy and mountain hiking to scuba diving and hunting expeditions - with education on nutrition and healthy living. These retreats include holistic dietitians, counselors, and subject matter experts in each therapeutic modality.

Common Inquiries

What does your foundation do?

We provide holistic wellness retreats for veterans, first responders, and their families dealing with TBI and PTSD. Our foundation funds these retreats through grants, making them accessible to those who need them most.

Who is eligible for retreat grants?

Veterans, first responders, and their family members or friends who are dealing with the effects of TBI or PTSD. We provide grants that cover the cost of attending our retreats, supporting relationships of all kinds - whether with family, friends, or loved ones.

How do I apply for a retreat grant?

To apply for a grant to attend our retreats, please use the Contact form below or email us directly at thestronghumans@gmail.com. Include information about your service background and how our programs could support your healing journey. We'll guide you through the application process.

Can I donate resources other than money?

Yes! We welcome donations of equipment, services, venue access, professional expertise, and other resources that support our retreats. Companies and individuals can contribute by providing therapy equipment, outdoor gear, nutritional supplies, or professional services. Please contact us to discuss how you can help.

What types of activities do you offer?

Our activity therapy programs are diverse and tailored to individual needs. We offer hiking, hunting, fishing, scuba diving, surfing, equine therapy, animal therapy, and more. Each retreat features different activities based on participant needs and seasonal availability.

What is your nutrition program?

Our comprehensive nutrition education covers sourcing farm-fresh foods and meal preparation. Participants learn from holistic dietitians about selecting quality ingredients, building relationships with local farmers, and preparing nutritious meals that support healing from TBI and PTSD.

Farm-Fresh Nutrition Education

1

Sourcing Farm-Fresh Foods

Learn how to identify, select, and source the highest quality farm-fresh ingredients. We teach participants how to build relationships with local farmers, navigate farmers' markets, and understand seasonal availability to ensure access to nutrient-dense, healing foods.

2

Meal Preparation Mastery

Our hands-on cooking workshops empower participants with practical skills to prepare nutritious, delicious meals using whole, unprocessed ingredients. From basic knife skills to advanced meal planning, we cover everything needed to maintain a healthy diet that supports mental and physical wellness.

3

Holistic Nutrition Guidance

Working with certified holistic dietitians, participants learn how proper nutrition can aid in managing PTSD and TBI symptoms. We focus on anti-inflammatory foods, gut-brain connection, and personalized nutrition plans that complement each individual's healing journey. We believe that food is medicineâ€”providing evidence-informed, whole-person nutritional strategies to support optimal health, resilience, and performance.
